Quick Facts About Japan

<>Japan is an archipelago nation (a chain of islands) located in East Asia,famous for being a unique blend of ancient tradition and ultra-modern technology.

Capital:
Tokyo (The largest city and one of the largest metropolitan areas in the world).
Geography:
Located in the Pacific Ocean, it consists of nearly 7,000 islands, with the four largest being Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku.
Culture & Technology:
Known for its strong sense of discipline, deep respect for others, and aesthetic concepts like "Wabi-Sabi." It is a global leader in robotics, automotive manufacturing, and electronics.
Landmarks:
The geography is characterized by mountainous terrain and volcanic activity; the most famous natural landmark is the sacred Mount Fuji.

<>Japanese cuisine (Washoku) is centered around rice, fish, and seafood, emphasizing simplicity and fresh, seasonal ingredients:

Sushi:
The most globally famous Japanese dish. It consists of vinegared rice that is rolled or pressed with raw slices of fish or seafood (like tuna and salmon) or vegetables.
Ramen:
A very popular comfort food that consists of wheat noodles served in a rich broth (typically flavored with soy sauce, miso, or bone stock), topped with slices of meat (often pork), eggs, and seaweed.
Tempura:
Seafood or vegetables lightly coated in a thin, chilled batter and deep-fried until light and crispy.
