Brief Facts About Egypt

<>Egypt, or the Arab Republic of Egypt, is a transcontinental country located in the northeastern corner of Africa, with an Asian extension into the Sinai Peninsula.

Capital:
Cairo.
Significance:
It holds one of the world's oldest and greatest civilizations, famous for ancient monuments like the Pyramids and the Sphinx.
Geography:
The Nile River is the country's main life artery, around which the vast majority of the population and agriculture are concentrated.Egypt is also home to the Suez Canal, a vital global shipping lane.

<>Egyptian cuisine is rich, relying heavily on grains, legumes, and vegetables. The three most famous national dishes are:

Koshari:
A popular main dish made from a hearty mix of macaroni, rice, and brown lentils, topped with chickpeas, crispy fried onions, and a spicy tomato sauce seasoned with garlic and vinegar.
Foul Medames (Fava Beans):
Considered the most important national dish, especially for breakfast. It consists of slow-cooked fava beans, typically seasoned with oil, cumin, and lemon.
Ta'ameya (Egyptian Falafel):
Deep-fried patties made from crushed fava beans (not chickpeas, like in the Levant), parsley, and spices. It's an indispensable part of breakfast and light dinners.

<>The current Egyptian flag consists of three horizontal, equal-sized bands with the Eagle of Saladin in the center. Each color symbolizes a historical stage or national value:

Red:
Symbolizes the struggle and sacrifices (blood of martyrs) Egypt endured during the revolutionary period before 1952,
White:
Symbolizes peace and the purity of intent following the 1952 revolution, marking the transition from monarchy to a republic.
Black:
Symbolizes the end of oppression and colonialism that the country suffered.
The Eagle (Golden Eagle):
The Eagle of Saladin is a symbol of power and the country's historical depth and strength.
